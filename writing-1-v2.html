~2 min · 30 words

2
Overview — the big picture ★ Most important
~3 min · 35 words
Summarise 2–3 main trends. No specific data. Start with "Overall," or "In general,". Never skip this paragraph.
3
Body paragraph 1
~6 min · 50 words
Describe the first major feature with specific data, comparisons, and precise language.
4
Body paragraph 2
~6 min · 45 words
Cover the second feature or contrasting trend. Include exceptions or notable details. No conclusion needed.
!
Never write a conclusion paragraph — the overview replaces it. Never add opinions or explain why the data looks the way it does. Your only job is accurate, organised description.

Task: The line graph below shows the number of visitors (in millions) to three London museums between 2010 and 2020. This is the same graph used in the Band 8 answer — follow each step below to see how that answer was built.
British Museum — starts high, stays highest, peaks 2018
Natural History Museum — middle, dips 2013–15, recovers
Science Museum — starts lowest, sharpest rise overall
1
Read & analyse the graph
Spend 3 minutes before writing anything
›
Discuss — what do you notice?
Look at this graph and ask yourself four questions before touching your pen:
① How many lines? Three — so you have three subjects to cover.
② What are the axes? X = years (2010–2020), Y = visitors in millions. Note the unit — you must include "million" in your answer.
③ What is the overall direction? All three lines rise. That is your overview statement.
④ What stands out? British Museum is consistently highest. Science Museum has the steepest growth. Natural History Museum has a dip around 2013. These are your body paragraph details.
What to do with this
Jot down: general trend (all rising) + 2 stand-out facts (BM highest, Science steepest). These two observations become your overview paragraph. Don't write yet — just note them down.
2
Write the Introduction
Paraphrase the task prompt — ~30 words, no data
›
Discuss — what does the prompt say?
The task says: "The line graph below shows the number of visitors to three museums in London between 2010 and 2020."

You must restate this in completely different words. Look at each key phrase and find a synonym or restructure it:
"shows" → illustrates / depicts / presents
"number of visitors" → how visitor numbers changed / visitor figures
"between 2010 and 2020" → over a ten-year period from 2010 to 2020
"three museums in London" → keep the names, but reorder the sentence
Band 8 sentence written
The line graph illustrates how visitor numbers changed at three London museums — the British Museum, the Natural History Museum, and the Science Museum — over a ten-year period from 2010 to 2020.
Why it scores well: "shows" replaced by "illustrates how … changed" — a structural paraphrase, not just a word swap. The museums are named (adds detail). The time period is reworded. No data mentioned. ✓
3
Write the Overview
The most important paragraph — 2 main trends, no figures
›
Discuss — what are the 2 biggest things you see?
Look at the graph again. Squint so you ignore the small details. What are the two most obvious things a first-time reader would notice?
Thing 1: All three lines go up — so "overall growth" is the main pattern.
Thing 2: The British Museum is always at the top. The Science Museum has the most dramatic rise. These are the two stand-outs worth highlighting.

What NOT to write here: No numbers like "5.8 million" or "2010". The overview is the big picture — data goes in the body.
Band 8 overview written
Overall, all three museums experienced growth in visitor numbers throughout the period, though at varying rates. The British Museum consistently attracted the greatest number of visitors, while the Science Museum recorded the most dramatic increase of the three.
Why it scores well: Starts with "Overall," ✓ — Two observations stated ✓ — No specific figures ✓ — "though at varying rates" adds nuance without data ✓ — Both stand-out facts from step 1 are included ✓
4
Write Body Paragraph 1
Group similar lines — British Museum + Natural History Museum
›
Discuss — which lines are similar?
Look at the graph. The British Museum and Natural History Museum both follow a similar upward pattern. The Science Museum is the odd one out — it rises far more steeply from a much lower base. So the logical grouping is:
Body 1: British Museum + Natural History Museum (similar upward trend, both high)
Body 2: Science Museum (most dramatic, contrasting trajectory)

For each line in Body 1, use this formula: start value + year → key change → end value + year. For the NHM, also mention the dip — it is a notable feature that shows careful reading.
Band 8 Body 1 written
The British Museum remained the most visited institution throughout. Starting at approximately 5.8 million in 2010, visitor numbers rose steadily to reach a peak of nearly 7.4 million in 2018, before declining slightly towards 2020. The Natural History Museum followed a broadly similar upward trajectory, growing from 4.2 million to around 5.9 million, although it experienced a brief dip between 2013 and 2015 before recovering strongly.
Why it scores well: Formula applied (start → peak → end) ✓ — "rose steadily" and "a peak of" are precise trend vocabulary ✓ — NHM described with "broadly similar" linking it to BM ✓ — Dip flagged as an exception ✓ — Both lines covered without repeating the same structure ✓
5
Write Body Paragraph 2
The contrasting outlier — Science Museum's dramatic rise
›
Discuss — what makes the Science Museum different?
Look at the green line. Three things make it worth a separate paragraph:
① It starts the lowest of all three (2.1 million in 2010)
② It rises the most steeply — more than doubling by 2020
③ Despite that growth, it remains the least visited throughout

This contrast — dramatic growth AND still lowest — is the interesting story. Your paragraph should capture all three elements. Use "In contrast" to signal the shift from Body 1.
Band 8 Body 2 written
In contrast, the Science Museum showed the most notable growth of all three institutions. Beginning from a comparatively modest base of 2.1 million in 2010, figures climbed sharply, more than doubling to approximately 4.6 million by 2020. This steep upward trajectory meant that, although it remained the least visited of the three, it closed the gap considerably with the other museums by the end of the period.
Why it scores well: "In contrast" links to Body 1 ✓ — "more than doubling" is stronger than just listing two figures ✓ — "although it remained the least visited" shows the nuance ✓ — "closed the gap considerably" ties back to the overview's "varying rates" ✓ — No conclusion paragraph needed ✓
★The "more than doubling" technique: Instead of writing "it went from 2.1M to 4.6M", write "more than doubling from 2.1M to 4.6M". You express the same data AND give a ratio comparison in one clause — this is what separates band 7 from band 8 answers.

Band 8 Sample Answer
~185 words
Introduction

The line graph illustrates how visitor numbers changed at three London museums — the British Museum, the Natural History Museum, and the Science Museum — over a ten-year period from 2010 to 2020.

Overview

Overall, all three museums experienced growth in visitor numbers throughout the period, though at varying rates. The British Museum consistently attracted the greatest number of visitors, while the Science Museum recorded the most dramatic increase of the three.

Body 1

The British Museum remained the most visited institution throughout. Starting at approximately 5.8 million in 2010, visitor numbers rose steadily to reach a peak of nearly 7.4 million in 2018, before declining slightly towards 2020. The Natural History Museum followed a broadly similar upward trajectory, growing from 4.2 million to around 5.9 million, although it experienced a brief dip between 2013 and 2015 before recovering strongly.

Body 2

In contrast, the Science Museum showed the most notable growth of all three institutions. Beginning from a comparatively modest base of 2.1 million in 2010, figures climbed sharply, more than doubling to approximately 4.6 million by 2020. This steep upward trajectory meant that, although it remained the least visited of the three, it closed the gap considerably with the other museums by the end of the period.

Bar Chart

Compares categories or shows change across discrete periods. Focus on highest, lowest, and notable differences.

Comparison language

The UK recorded the highest figure at 78%, more than double the proportion seen in Germany (34%), while France and Spain showed broadly similar levels at approximately 55% and 52% respectively.
Task: The bar chart below shows the percentage of adults who used the internet daily in five European countries in 2022. This is the same chart used in the Band 8 answer — follow each step to see how the response was built.
1
Read & analyse the chart
Identify type, extremes, and groupings before writing
›
Discuss — what do you notice?
Ask four questions immediately:
① Simple or grouped bars? Simple — one bar per country. Easier to describe.
② One time period or multiple? One snapshot (2022). Use past simple tense throughout.
③ Highest and lowest? Norway (95%) and Italy (61%) — these go in the overview.
④ Natural groupings? Norway, Sweden, UK all cluster above 85%. France and Italy both sit noticeably lower. This grouping drives your two body paragraphs.
What to note down
Overview facts: All countries high, Norway highest, Italy lowest.
Body 1 group: Norway (95%) + Sweden (92%) + UK (87%) — all above 85%.
Body 2 group: France (76%) + Italy (61%) — noticeably lower.
2
Write the Introduction
Paraphrase the task — reword every key phrase
›
Discuss — how to paraphrase this prompt
Task says: "shows the percentage of adults who used the internet daily in five European countries in 2022."
"shows" → illustrates
"percentage of adults" → proportion of adults
"used the internet daily" → accessed the internet on a daily basis
"in 2022" → keep — it is a specific year, not much to change
Band 8 sentence written
The bar chart illustrates the proportion of adults in five European countries who accessed the internet on a daily basis in 2022.
Why it works: Clean, accurate paraphrase. No data. One sentence is enough for an introduction. ✓
3
Write the Overview
General pattern + two stand-out facts, no figures
›
Discuss — what are the two biggest observations?
Observation 1: All five countries have high usage — the bars are all tall. State this as the general pattern.
Observation 2: Norway is clearly the tallest bar, Italy is clearly the shortest. Name these two extremes in the overview — no figures yet.
Band 8 overview written
Overall, internet usage was high across all five nations, with the majority of adults using the internet daily. Norway recorded the highest figure by a considerable margin, while Italy had the lowest rate of daily usage.
Why it works: General statement first ✓ — Two extremes named ✓ — "by a considerable margin" adds strength without needing a number ✓ — No figures ✓
4
Write Body Paragraph 1
The high-usage group — Norway, Sweden, UK
›
Discuss — how to describe a group of bars
Norway (95%), Sweden (92%) and the UK (87%) are clearly a cluster. The technique is: name the highest → name the others in descending order → make a group observation.
Step A: Start with the highest — Norway at 95%
Step B: Add the others — "followed closely by Sweden (92%) and UK (87%)"
Step C: Group observation — "all exceeded 85%" — this ties the three together as a meaningful cluster, not just a list
Band 8 Body 1 written
Norway had the highest proportion of daily internet users at 95%, followed closely by Sweden at 92% and the UK at 87%. These three countries all exceeded 85%, suggesting consistently high levels of internet adoption in northern and western Europe.
Why it works: Highest named first ✓ — "followed closely by" avoids repetition of "had" ✓ — Group threshold (85%) ties them together ✓ — Final clause adds geographic insight ✓
5
Write Body Paragraph 2
The lower group — France & Italy, with a proportional comparison
›
Discuss — what is the most interesting thing about France and Italy?
France is 76% — noticeably lower than the UK but not drastically different.
Italy is 61% — clearly the outlier. This is the most striking single figure in the chart.

The key technique here: instead of just saying "Italy had 61%", compare it to Norway: 61% is roughly two-thirds of 95%. Writing "approximately two-thirds of Norway's figure" is far more impressive than listing the two numbers separately.
Band 8 Body 2 written
By contrast, France and Italy recorded noticeably lower figures, at 76% and 61% respectively. Italy's rate was particularly striking, standing at approximately two-thirds of Norway's figure, and represented the only country where fewer than two-thirds of adults used the internet daily.
Why it works: "By contrast" links back to Body 1 ✓ — "respectively" handles two figures elegantly ✓ — Proportional comparison ("two-thirds of Norway") is the band 8 technique ✓ — Final clause ("only country where") adds a notable observation ✓
★The proportional comparison rule: Whenever two bars are significantly different, express the gap as a ratio — "double", "three times as high", "two-thirds of". This is the single technique that most reliably separates Band 7 from Band 8 bar chart answers.

Pie Chart

Shows proportions of a whole at one moment in time. Never use trend verbs — only proportion language.

Words to avoid in pie charts

Task: The pie chart shows the percentage breakdown of household expenditure in a typical UK family in 2022. Follow each step to see how the Band 8 answer was built from this chart.
1
Read & analyse the chart
One pie or two? Rank segments before writing
›
Discuss — what do you see?
① One pie or two? One pie — so describe proportions only. Never use trend verbs (rose/fell).
② Rank the segments: Housing (35%) → Food (25%) → Transport (18%) → Education (12%) → Other (10%)
③ Natural groups: Housing + Food are both large (35%, 25%). Transport is mid-size. Education + Other are both small and close (12%, 10%).
④ Stand-out feature: Housing dominates at over a third. Education + Other together are still less than Housing alone — a strong comparison.
Paragraph plan
Overview: Housing dominant, Other smallest.
Body 1: Housing (35%) + Food (25%) — the two big segments, together 60%.
Body 2: Transport (18%) + Education (12%) + Other (10%) — the three smaller segments grouped.
2
Write the Introduction
Reword the task without data
›
Discuss — paraphrase approach
"shows" → illustrates
"percentage breakdown of household expenditure" → how a typical UK household divided its spending
"in a typical UK family" → already integrated above
"in 2022" → keep as is
Band 8 sentence written
The pie chart illustrates how a typical UK household divided its spending across five major categories in 2022.
Why it works: "divided its spending" is a strong structural rewrite of "percentage breakdown of expenditure" ✓ — Concise at one sentence ✓ — No data ✓
3
Write the Overview
Dominant segment + smallest segment — no figures
›
Discuss — what two things stand out?
Thing 1: Housing is by far the biggest slice — it visually dominates the chart.
Thing 2: Other is the smallest segment at the opposite extreme.

These two observations — biggest and smallest — are always the safest overview for a pie chart. No numbers needed.
Band 8 overview written
Overall, Housing was by far the dominant expenditure category, accounting for over a third of total spending. At the other extreme, Other costs represented the smallest proportion.
Why it works: "by far the dominant" is stronger than "the largest" ✓ — "over a third" is a fraction, not an exact % ✓ — "At the other extreme" elegantly signals contrast ✓ — Two facts, no figures ✓
4
Write Body Paragraph 1
The two big segments — mix % with fractions
›
Discuss — the fraction + % technique
Housing is 35% and Food is 25%. Together they are 60%. Look at the key technique:
Instead of: "Housing was 35% and Food was 25%."
Write: "Housing constituted 35%, or just over a third… Food represented approximately one quarter (25%)… together responsible for three-fifths."

You use the same two numbers but express them three ways: as %, as a fraction, and as a combined fraction. This is what earns Lexical Resource Band 8.
Band 8 Body 1 written
Housing constituted the largest share at 35%, or just over a third of total household expenditure. Food was the second largest category, representing approximately one quarter (25%) of spending, making these two items collectively responsible for three-fifths of all expenditure.
Why it works: "35%, or just over a third" — % and fraction in same clause ✓ — "approximately one quarter (25%)" — fraction first, % in parentheses ✓ — "three-fifths" combines both segments ✓ — Three different vocabulary choices for proportion ✓
★Pie chart rule #1: Never use "increased" or "decreased" — pie charts are a single point in time. The moment you write a trend verb, you lose Task Achievement marks.
5
Write Body Paragraph 2
Group the three smaller segments together
›
Discuss — how to handle multiple small segments
Transport (18%), Education (12%) and Other (10%) together make 40%. Rather than describing each in its own sentence, group them. Then add one strong comparative observation: these three combined are still less than Housing alone (35% vs 40% — close, but makes a good contrast point).

The key insight for the final sentence: Housing alone (35%) nearly equals all three smaller segments combined (40%). This kind of synthesised comparison is what pushes an answer to band 8.
Band 8 Body 2 written
Transport accounted for nearly a fifth of expenditure at 18%, while Education and Other made up the remaining 22% in roughly equal shares of 12% and 10% respectively. These three smaller categories combined still amounted to less than Housing alone, underscoring its dominance in household budgets.
Why it works: "nearly a fifth" is a fraction for 18% ✓ — "roughly equal shares" groups Education + Other ✓ — Final sentence is a synthesised comparison, not raw data ✓ — "underscoring its dominance" ties back to the overview ✓

Discuss — what kind of process is this?
① How many stages? Five — state this in your overview ("five distinct stages").
② Linear or cyclical? Linear — it has a clear start (rainfall) and a clear end (distribution to homes). Use "finally" for the last stage.
③ Natural or manufacturing? Natural/civic — use present simple passive ("is collected", "is pumped", "is filtered"). Never past tense here.
④ What are stages 1–2 vs 3–5? Stages 1–2 = collection and transport. Stages 3–5 = purification and delivery. This gives you a natural Body 1 / Body 2 split.

Discuss — sequencing without repetition
The biggest mistake in process answers is using "then" for every stage: "Then it is pumped. Then it is filtered. Then..." This kills your Coherence score. You need a different connector for each stage:
Stage 1: "Initially," — signals the start
Stage 2: "It is then pumped" — "then" is fine once
Stage 3: "At the next stage," or "At which point" — signals a new step

Each stage should also be passive: "is collected", "is pumped", "is passed through".
Band 8 Body 1 written
Initially, rainwater is collected in a large reservoir, where it is stored until required. It is then pumped to a water treatment facility, at which point the purification process begins. At the next stage, the water is passed through layers of sand, which remove larger particles and impurities through physical filtration.
Why it works: "Initially" → "then" → "At the next stage" — three different connectors ✓ — All passive ✓ — "where it is stored until required" adds detail about Stage 1 ✓ — "which remove larger particles" explains the purpose of Stage 3 ✓ — Explaining the purpose of a stage (not just describing it) shows high-level writing ✓

Discuss — how to end a process description
Stage 4 is the chemical treatment (chlorine). Stage 5 is distribution. You can add the safety testing as an implicit sub-stage.

Key grammar upgrade: Use a participial clause — "Having been treated in this way, the water is tested..." — instead of "After it is treated, the water is tested." This is a more complex structure that the grammar criterion rewards.

Always end with "Finally," for the last stage — it signals to the examiner you have covered the full process.
Band 8 Body 2 written
Following filtration, the water undergoes chemical treatment, during which chlorine is added to eliminate any remaining bacteria and microorganisms. Having been treated in this way, the water is tested to ensure it meets safety standards. Finally, it is distributed through an underground pipe network to homes and businesses, completing the process.
Why it works: "Following filtration" links cleanly to Body 1 ✓ — "during which chlorine is added" is a sophisticated relative clause ✓ — "Having been treated" is a participial clause — advanced grammar ✓ — "meets safety standards" adds real-world process knowledge ✓ — "Finally" clearly signals the last stage ✓ — "completing the process" ties back to the overview ✓
